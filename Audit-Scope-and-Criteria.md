# Defining Audit Scope and Criteria

**Reference:** ISO/IEC 27001:2022, Clause 9.2 (Internal Audit)
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## Why This Comes First

Before you audit anything, you need to agree what you are auditing against and how far it extends. Skipping this step is the most common reason internal audits produce weak, indefensible results. An auditor who walks in without a documented scope ends up improvising criteria mid-audit, which undermines the credibility of every finding that follows.

---

## Defining Audit Scope

Document the following before the audit begins:

| Element | What to Define |
|---|---|
| Organisational scope | Which business units, departments, or locations are included |
| System scope | Which systems, applications, and infrastructure fall within the ISMS boundary |
| Process scope | Which business processes are covered (e.g. software development, customer support, HR onboarding) |
| Exclusions | Anything explicitly excluded from the ISMS scope, and the justification for exclusion |
| Time period | The period being audited (e.g. since the last internal audit, or the full certification cycle) |

### Practical Note

Your audit scope should match your organisation's documented ISMS scope statement (required under Clause 4.3). If your internal audit scope is broader or narrower than the certified ISMS scope without clear justification, this itself becomes a finding.

---

## Defining Audit Criteria

Audit criteria are the standards against which you are measuring. For an ISO 27001 internal audit, this typically includes:

| Criteria Source | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| ISO/IEC 27001:2022 | Clauses 4-10 and Annex A controls |
| Statement of Applicability (SoA) | Which Annex A controls the organisation has determined are applicable, and why |
| Internal policies and procedures | The organisation's own documented ISMS policies, which the audit checks for both existence and actual adherence |
| Legal and regulatory requirements | Any additional legal, regulatory, or contractual requirements the organisation has committed to (e.g. NDPA, GDPR, sector-specific regulation) |
| Previous audit findings | Outstanding nonconformities or corrective actions from prior audits or management reviews |

## Independence Requirement

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.2.2 requires that auditors do not audit their own work. If your organisation is small and the same person manages the ISMS and would also conduct the audit, you need an external or otherwise independent auditor, this is not optional.
